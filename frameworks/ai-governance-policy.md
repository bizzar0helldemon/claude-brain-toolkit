## Quality Control

Quality control maps directly to the Discernment competency in the [[AI Fluency Framework]].

### The Verification Loop

> *Guidance: Define the steps every AI-assisted deliverable passes through before it ships. Adapt the loop below to your workflow.*

Every AI-assisted deliverable passes through this cycle:

1. **Generate** — AI produces initial output based on your Description
2. **Evaluate** — You assess accuracy, relevance, tone, and completeness (Product Discernment)
3. **Refine** — Iterate with AI or manually edit to address gaps
4. **Verify** — Final human review before delivery, with specific attention to:
   - Factual accuracy (especially claims, numbers, citations)
   - Appropriateness for the audience
   - Consistency with brand/voice requirements
   - No hallucinated content passed off as fact
5. **Vouch** — You put your name on it. If you wouldn't stand behind it, it doesn't ship.

## Decision-Making Criteria for Ethical Dilemmas

When facing an uncertain situation, work through these questions:

1. **Who is affected?** List all stakeholders — client, their customers, employees, end users, the public
2. **What's the worst case?** If this goes wrong, what's the actual harm? To whom?
3. **Would I be comfortable if this were public?** If my process and AI involvement were fully visible, would I be proud of how I handled it?
4. **What would the affected parties want?** If I asked the people impacted, what would they expect or prefer?
5. **Does this align with my stated principles?** Check against the core principles above
6. **When in doubt, choose transparency** — it's almost always better to over-disclose than under-disclose

## Disclosure Practices

### General Principle

Disclose AI involvement to the degree that the audience needs to make informed decisions about the work. The goal is trust, not performative transparency.

### Disclosure Levels

| Level | When to Use | What to Say |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **Full disclosure** | Academic work, published writing, regulated industries, client contracts that require it | Detailed statement of what AI did, which tools, what human review occurred |
| **Standard disclosure** | Most client work, professional deliverables | Brief note that AI tools assisted in creation, with human review confirmed |
| **Contextual disclosure** | Creative work, internal tools, social media | Mention AI involvement when relevant or when asked |
| **Implicit** | Personal productivity, brainstorming, research | No disclosure needed — this is your own workflow |

### When More Detailed Disclosure Is Appropriate

- The audience has explicitly asked about AI involvement
- The work will be published, cited, or used as an authoritative source
- Regulatory or contractual requirements demand it
- The work involves claims of expertise, originality, or personal experience
- AI played a substantial role in the creative or analytical output (not just formatting or editing)
- The content could influence important decisions (financial, health, legal, safety)
